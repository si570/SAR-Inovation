iDrone

Frame: Usually constructed from lightweight materials like carbon fiber or plastic, the frame serves as the structure that holds the essential parts together. Propellers and Motors: Drones are propelled by multiple rotors powered by electric motors. These motors provide the necessary thrust for flight and maneuverability. Flight Controller: An onboard computer system manages the drone's flight by processing commands from the pilot or autopilot software. It adjusts the speed of individual motors to control movement. Sensors and Cameras: Many drones are equipped with sensors like GPS, accelerometers, gyroscopes, and altimeters for stabilization, navigation, and collecting data. Additionally, cameras or other imaging devices capture photos and videos. Battery: Drones rely on rechargeable lithium-polymer batteries for power, determining their flight duration. Advanced drones might have swappable or larger capacity batteries.
